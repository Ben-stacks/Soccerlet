Who will win the FIFA world cup this time?


List of 7 countries that may likely win the FIFA World Cup in Qatar 2022.

1. Belgium
It is well known that Belgium’s best stars dominated the club side landscape for the past 4 seasons. Some key Players like Eden Hazard, Kevin De Bruyne and Thibaut Courtois earned world-class recognitions for years in tournaments.

Clearly the Belgium national are bringing a strong squad to Qatar. Though Eden Hazard never looked like the player that get better highlight in Real Madrid but It all comes down to fitness and form. Players like Kevin De Bruyne, Romelu Lukaku, Youri Tielemans seems to have confidence in national side.

If this golden generation of Belgium can put it together for what could be one last ride, there is no reason to think it cannot win its first major trophy.



2. France
The defending champions were consist of young and prime players when they lifted France’s second World Cup trophy.
Kylian Mbappe drew comparisons to Pelé at young age and this season he was at prime in PSG performance at ucl. Hugo Lloris and his defense conceded just six goals, with half of those coming in the round of 16 thriller against Argentina in the last world cup. Paul Pogba and N’golo Kante bossed the midfield each game. Now all those pieces will return to Qatar 2022.

France also adds some of the most in-form players in the world. RB Leipzig’s Christopher Nkunku comes in. Plus, Karim Benzema, perhaps the favorite for the 2022 Ballon d’Or, returned to France’s national team.

France won the last UEFA Nations League, and with this shapes they are likely the favorites to lift the world cup again in Qatar.

3. England
England has the second-youngest squad in the tournament at an average age of 26. With the depth across the field everywhere except for perhaps center-back, England should be among the contenders to lift another World Cup trophy.

With depth across the field everywhere except for perhaps center-back, having reached the semi-finals at the previous World Cup, they have won knockout matches at consecutive tournaments. England should be among the contenders to lift another World Cup trophy.

The most successful country when it comes to the World Cup looks more confident and convinced for another run. Brazil stormed through CONMEBOL World Cup qualifying. Neymar finished second in goals scored as Brazil did not lose a single game.

With Neymar looking strong, it is encourages Brazilian fans and also to know that Vinicius is having a good season at Real Madrid. Also, Fabinho, Gabriel Jesus and others plays more confident in roles during World Cups. Brazil is No. 1 in the FIFA World Rankings. 5. Spain
In Euro 2020 Spain were beaten on spot-kicks against Italy after scraping past Switzerland on penalties. They have been known for tactical and time confident in world cup stage.

However, they have managed to build a team reliable to haul a way through in a big game. Hopefully before next November, Spain will very much be back in business.

6. Argentina
Argentina like their south American counterpart Brazil went through CONMEBOL World Cup Qualifying without a loss. They are also the last side to beat Brazil in a big competition.

This may be Lionel Messi’s final World Cup after coming so close in 2014. In 2018 Argentina loss against France in round of 16. In that match, Argentina looked like a threatening side but France was overwhelmingly good.

Now with the Argentine current big moment success they have a team strong and determined to reach the knockout stages. It will come down to Messi’s wonderful performance on the big stage for Argentina to have any chance.

7. Portugal
In the last Europian cup, Portugal lost their crown, but Cristiano took home the Golden Boot with five goals and an assist. The Man United forward contributed in keeping Portugal in contention through the round of 16 which also has been the case all along their competition.

Portugal went through play-off and came out with outstanding perfomance which lead to their ticket to Qatar. Its taken to be a ready team who pulled from play off so easily and are eager to extend to big time in world cup.

In Qatar, Ronaldo may be less mobile but will certainly be up front and centre, with the likes of Fernandes, Jota, Felix etc they may takes a final shot at World Cup glory.
